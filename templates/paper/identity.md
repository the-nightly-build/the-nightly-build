# paper

A paper article reports on one research paper: what it claims, how the claim
is earned, and where the claim stops. Its subject is the paper, never the
experience of reading about it.

Open with the source. The `abstract` section carries the paper card (title,
authors, venue, link) and the abstract verbatim, so the original's own words
are on record before any commentary.

Rebuild the argument in your own words, your own examples, and the order that
teaches best; the original's order rarely is. Define each concept at the
moment the argument first spends it. Anchor the moments the argument turns on
with `nb-excerpt`: the original's exact sentence, an honest locator, and a
one-line gloss of what it does and does not claim.

Weigh the evidence as a reviewer would: what was measured, on what, and what
would count as the result failing to generalize. Name a weak evaluation
plainly. Place the paper among the work it builds on and the work that
disagrees with it, and state a verdict on its claims before the sources.
