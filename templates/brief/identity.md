# brief

A brief works like a wire service. Write for a reader who already saw the headline
and add what it left out: the number, the caveat, the fact that decides whether it
matters.

Each item is a judgment about why something matters, not a recap of what happened.
Give the development, a link to its primary source (the original announcement or
paper, over any coverage), and a why-it-matters line, the required `data-nb-why`.
Cover only what actually moved; a short honest brief beats a padded one, and
inventing an item to hit a count is a failure.

When a story you have covered develops, say so and build on it. Continuity turns a
nightly list into a running record.
