# brief — editorial brief

The registry entry gives this template its geometry. This brief gives it its
identity: what a great brief actually does. It is composed as a layer, so a series
prompt can specialize it. It never overrides the house floor.

A brief is a wire service, not a link dump. Write for a reader who already saw the
headline: your job is to add what the headline left out. The number. The caveat.
The thing that makes it real, or makes it noise.

Every item is a judgment about why something matters, not a restatement of what
happened. Each carries the thing itself, a link to its primary source (cite the
original announcement or paper over any coverage of it), and a why-it-matters line
that earns its place. Cover only what actually moved; a short honest brief beats a
padded one, and inventing an item to reach a count is a failure, not a save.

When a story you have covered before develops, say so and build on it. Continuity
is what turns a nightly list into a running record.
