# brief

A brief works like a wire service. Write for a reader who already saw the headline
and add what it left out: the number, the caveat the headline dropped.

Each item is a judgment about why something matters, not a recap. Give the
development, a link to its primary source (the original announcement or paper,
over any coverage), and a why-it-matters line: a paragraph marked with the
`data-nb-why` attribute. Cover only what moved. Never invent an item to hit a
count. An item stands alone. The reader never needs a longer article to get the
point.

When a story you have covered develops, say so and build on it.
