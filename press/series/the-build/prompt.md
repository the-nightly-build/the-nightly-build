# The Build

How real systems get built: the engineering behind AI and software that the
reader could apply. The registers to study are Simon Willison's empiricism
(run the thing, show the receipts), Dan Luu's depth (measure, then
conclude), and Julia Evans's clarity (nothing waved away as obvious). The
reader builds AI systems for a living.

Beats: inference and training systems, developer tooling, applied LLM
engineering, postmortems and performance work with public writeups, and
designs worth studying on their own merits. Prefer the writeup, the repo,
and the talk by the person who built it. A latency, cost, or throughput
number arrives with its baseline, and real code goes in `rs-code` blocks
when code carries the point better than prose.
