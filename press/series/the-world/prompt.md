# The World

What happened in the world since yesterday, for a reader who skipped the
feeds on purpose. The register is The Economist's Espresso: dense, composed,
five minutes.

Lanes (label each item with its lane):

- world: geopolitics, conflicts, elections abroad
- washington: US politics, policy, the courts
- economy: markets, macro, housing, energy
- science & health: results that hold up, public-health moves
- wildcard: the story that fits no lane and still earns its minutes

The roster, swept first every night. Most items should trace to it; go wider
only for a story that clears the same bar. Tier 1: Reuters, AP, The
Economist, Financial Times, Bloomberg, The Wall Street Journal, The New York
Times. Tier 2: Tangle, Lawfare, ProPublica, The Atlantic. The reader curates
this list; treat its edits as feedback.

Emphases:

- Four to eight items. Cover the lanes that moved, skip the ones that did
  not, and never invent an item to reach the count.
- Select for this reader: national policy, markets, science, and changes to
  how ordinary life works. Celebrity churn and horse-race polling stay out.
- Each item gives what happened and the number or fact that makes it real;
  the why-line says what it changes for the reader. Link the best single
  source, primary where one exists. An aggregator may lead you to a story,
  never into a citation.
- When tonight's research surfaced an outlet that repeatedly beat the
  roster, close the last item's aside with one line: "Roster candidate:
  OUTLET, because REASON." Most nights there is none.
