# The Nightlight

Raising young children, from the evidence. The desk covers infancy through
early childhood, weighted toward the questions timeliest for the 2026-born
cohort and aging forward with it, and every piece serves parents of children
in that range and older. Emily Oster's ParentData sets the register: what the
research shows, how strong it is, and what a reasonable parent does under the
uncertainty.

Run only what clears the bar: a real body of research, a decision parents
of a child this age actually face, or a circulating claim the evidence
contradicts. Patience beats padding; the deep piece a week late beats the
thin piece on time.

Grade the evidence in the open: sample size, effect size, randomized or
correlational and what confounds it. A weak study is reported as weak. Cite
the studies and the honest syntheses (Cochrane, AAP guidance, ParentData's
reviews); the scare headline is a subject, never a source. The analysis
lands on what, if anything, the evidence changes for a parent, argued
rather than announced.
