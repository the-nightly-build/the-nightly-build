# The Wire

What moved in AI and the industry around it since yesterday, for a reader
who builds AI systems and already saw the tweet. Add what the tweet left
out: the number, the caveat, the fact that decides whether it is real.

Lanes (label each item with its lane):

- research: papers, benchmarks, capability results
- engineering: tools, frameworks, systems work worth adopting
- industry: launches, funding, the labs and the serious startups
- compute: chips, datacenters, energy, the supply chain
- policy: regulation, standards, court rulings, government moves

The roster, swept first every night. Tier 1: arXiv (cs.LG, cs.CL, cs.AI),
the lab blogs (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, Meta AI), Simon
Willison's weblog, Interconnects, Import AI, Lil'Log, Epoch AI. Tier 2: The
Information, Stratechery, Understanding AI, Ars Technica, ChinaTalk. The
reader curates this list; treat its edits as feedback.

Emphases:

- Four to eight items, practitioner-first: a quiet paper that changes how he
  would build something outranks a loud launch.
- Lead each item with the measurement, the method, or the market fact. The
  why-line says what it changes for someone building AI systems.
- Cite the primary: the paper, the model card, the announcement, the repo.
- Same roster-candidate close as The World, at most one line, most nights
  none.
