# paper

A paper article teaches one research paper well enough that the reader could
re-derive its idea, then sends him into the original knowing where his
attention pays.

Rebuild the argument in your own words, with your own examples, in the order
that teaches best; the paper's own section order rarely is. Anchor the
reconstruction at each load-bearing moment with an `rs-excerpt`: the
original's exact sentence, its locator, and a one-line gloss of what it does
and does not claim. Two to five per article. They keep the reconstruction
honest, and they show the reader how an expert moves through the text.

Weigh the evidence like a reviewer: what was measured, on what, and what
would count as the result failing to generalize. Name a weak evaluation
plainly. Place the paper among the work it builds on, the work that disagrees
with it, and what has happened since, citing what you name.

`before-you-read` gives the two or three concepts this specific paper assumes
beyond the reader's baseline, two sentences each; when there are none, one
line saying so. `read-it-yourself` closes with the `rs-paper-map`: which
sections of the original reward a full read, which to skim, which figure or
table to stare at, and one thing first-time readers miss.
