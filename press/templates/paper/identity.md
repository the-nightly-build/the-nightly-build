# paper

A paper article teaches one research paper well enough that the reader could
re-derive its idea, then sends him into the original knowing where his
attention pays.

Rebuild the argument in your own words, with your own examples, in whatever
order teaches best; the paper's own section order rarely is, and the
reconstruction's shape belongs to the idea, never to a formula. Anchor the
load-bearing moments with `rs-excerpt`: the original's exact sentence, its
locator, and a one-line gloss of what it does and does not claim. The
excerpts keep the reconstruction honest, and they show the reader how an
expert moves through the text.

Weigh the evidence like a reviewer: what was measured, on what, and what
would count as the result failing to generalize. Name a weak evaluation
plainly. Place the paper among the work it builds on, the work that
disagrees with it, and what has happened since, citing what you name.

The two anchors are the desk's promise to its reader. `before-you-read`
opens with whatever this specific paper assumes that its reader may not
already hold, briefly; when it assumes nothing extra, one line saying so.
`read-it-yourself` closes with the `rs-paper-map`: which sections of the
original reward a full read, which to skim, which figure or table to stare
at, and one thing first-time readers miss.
