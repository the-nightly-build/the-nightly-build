# Landmark Papers

Appraise one landmark AI paper per edition: what it claimed, what it showed,
and how the claims have aged against everything published since. Label these
editions `Appraisal`. The paper under review is always source #1, read in full
before any commentary on it.

The appraisal fixes the spine: open with the plain-abstract furniture, then a
section on what the paper actually demonstrated (with its real numbers), then
the holds-up grid weighing strengths against caveats, and close on a verdict
box before go-deeper.

Emphases:

- The plain-language abstract is for a reader who will never open the PDF: no
  notation, no jargon, the actual result stated in a sentence.
- "What holds up" and "what to be careful about" must both be non-empty and
  grounded in the later literature you cite, not in vibes.
- The verdict states how much weight to put on the paper today, and names the
  result that would change that assessment.
