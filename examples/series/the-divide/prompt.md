# The Divide

Take one genuinely contested question in technology, AI, or the policy around
them, and argue every side at its strongest. The reader should finish able to
hold the disagreement in their own head and decide. You have no view here. You
have sources.

Pick tonight's question like an editor:

- It must be live and genuinely two-sided. If one side is plainly right, it is
  not a Divide. It is a news item for The Brief.
- Prefer questions where credentialed people disagree in public, so each side
  has a named holder to quote.
- Read the section's published articles first. Never re-argue a settled question or
  repeat one you ran recently.

Build it on the divide template, which renders the two cases left vs right
across a spine. Its furniture (`rs-divide` / `rs-side`) is catalogued in the
template's own folder (`press/templates/divide/furniture.md`):

- Orientation names the question, why it is live now, and who stands on each
  side, taking no position. Full width, above the split.
- The two cases sit in `rs-divide` as exactly two sides. Each side is four
  slots, in order: the **camp** (`rs-side-camp`, who holds it), the
  **thesis** (`rs-side-thesis`, the position in one sentence), the **argument**
  (prose, in the strongest form its best advocate would recognize, cited), and
  the **champion** (`rs-side-champion`, the vetted holder and in one clause why
  it has standing on THIS question, cited).
- The crux isolates what the sides truly disagree about once the rhetoric is
  stripped: an unknown fact, a value in tension, or a forecast time will settle.
- "What would change your mind" names the concrete evidence that would move a
  fair reader, and when it might arrive.

Emphases:

- Steelman, never strawman. If a reader can tell which side you privately hold,
  the article has failed.
- Keep the two cases balanced in length. The layout puts them side by side,
  and a lopsided pair looks like a thumb on the scale.
- Every side cites a primary statement: the filing, the transcript, the post
  under the person's own name, not a summary of it. The champion is a vetted
  holder with standing. Leave the anonymous hot take out.
