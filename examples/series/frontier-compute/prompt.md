# Frontier Compute

Deep dossiers on the companies that physically make the AI era possible: who
holds leverage over whom, why that leverage is hard to replicate, and what
would break it. Each edition is a dossier.

Shape the outline to the company, not to a template. A monopoly, a cyclical
commodity maker, and a shared bottleneck each want a different spine; the only
fixed points are an orienting open and a go-deeper close. A typical dossier
runs orientation, then two to four sections you name for this business, then
go-deeper.

Furniture that tends to earn its place here:

- A stat strip near the top: three or four load-bearing numbers (revenue mix,
  market share, capex, unit economics), each cited in nearby prose.
- A chart when a trend is the argument; keep it declarative (data-nb-chart).
- Position blocks when the bull and bear cases are genuinely contested, each
  stated at its strongest and attributed to a named holder of that view.

Emphases:

- The moat analysis is the core. Name the mechanism precisely (process lead,
  ecosystem lock-in, monopsony, standards capture) and quantify it. "Strong
  moat" is not analysis.
- Prefer primary sources: filings, earnings transcripts, technical docs. When
  a number carries weight, cite the filing, not the coverage of it.
- Steelman the bear case with the strongest named critic, never a strawman.
  A reader should not be able to tell which side you privately hold.
