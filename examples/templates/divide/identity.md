# divide

The manifest gives this template its geometry. This brief gives it its identity.
It composes as a layer, so the series prompt can specialize it. It never
overrides the house floor.

A Divide argues one genuinely contested question from every side at its
strongest. The reader finishes able to hold the disagreement in their own head
and decide. Bring sources and no view of your own.

Steelman, never strawman. If a reader can tell which side you privately hold,
the piece has failed. Keep the two cases balanced in length: the layout puts
them side by side, and an uneven pair reads as a verdict. Each side cites a
primary statement (the filing, the transcript, the post under the person's own
name), not a summary of it. Its champion is a vetted holder with standing, not
an anonymous hot take.

The crux is the whole point. Sort what still divides the sides into its kind (a
fact no one yet knows, a value in tension, or a forecast), say which this one
is, and name the concrete evidence that would settle it for an undecided
reader. Give every section a heading from this question's argument, not a stock
label. The skeleton's heading placeholders are instructions, not titles.
