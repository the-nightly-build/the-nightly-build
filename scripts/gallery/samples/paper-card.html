Communication-Deferred Training at Datacenter Scale

L. Osei, M. Tran, P. Varga · arXiv:2506.01847 · 2026

Read the paper
Large-scale language model training is increasingly constrained by facility power rather than accelerator supply. We show that deferring gradient synchronization to off-peak power windows reduces facility draw 47% on a 4,096-GPU cluster while costing 11% throughput, and characterize when the trade favors the deferral. Our scheduler treats the interconnect as a power market participant, batching all-reduce operations against a facility-level power budget and releasing them when marginal capacity is cheap. We evaluate across two cluster generations and three model scales, report end-to-end convergence parity with synchronous baselines, and release the scheduler and its traces. On a fully utilized site the recoverable overhead shrinks and the honest saving is closer to a third; we bound the conditions under which the method generalizes.1
